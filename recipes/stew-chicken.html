Brown Stew Chicken

Description

This Jamaican brown stew chiken is lighly fried, then braised down in a nice savoury sauce, which you can pour over your rice or whatever you are serving with this chicken dish. There is no time like the present to have one of Jamaica's favourite dishes.

Ingredients

  • 1.6kg whole chicken
  • 1½ tbsp demerara sugar
  • 400ml hot chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp browning sauce
  • 1 tsp plain flour mixed with 25ml cold water
  • 1-2 spring onions, sliced, to serve
  • rice and peas and slaw, to serve

For the marinade

  • 8 thyme sprigs
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • ½ scotch bonnet pepper, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose seasoning
  • 1 tbsp chicken seasoning
  • 6 whole pimento seeds, crushed
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Remove the skin from the chicken, joint it, cut the breasts in half and separate the legs and thighs.

  2. Put the chicken pieces in a large bowl along with all the marinade ingredients. Toss, then rub the mixture into the chicken. Cover tightly and chill for 24 hrs

  3. Heat the oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6. Tip the chicken and marinade into a deep ovenproof dish. Cover and bake for 40 mins, then remove the lid and stir in the sugar, stock, browning sauce and flour mixture. Bake for another 15 mins, uncovered, turning the chicken and basting it every 5 mins.

  4. ACheck the chicken is cooked through. If not, bake for 15 mins more, then check again. Taste for seasoning, then scatter with the spring onions and serve with rice and peas and slaw, if you like.
